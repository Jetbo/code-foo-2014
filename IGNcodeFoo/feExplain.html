Bay Bridge - Front End Explanation


Notes on method:

I used the JavaScript plugin Snap.svg to run my vector map app. I recreated the majority of lane diagram that was originally provided. Based upon research into the subject I was to find that the diagram isn't accurate and decided to change it to how it is shown on google maps (satellite view) instead.


List of changes:

  • All lanes and merge structures were updated
  • The incoming highway system was updated to be more accurate
  • FasTrak lanes were changed to match this
  • The delineator traffic posts seperating regular lanes from FasTrak lanes were also changed
  • The Rose Compass now points in the correct direction
  • Overall aesthetic improvements to increase legibility

List of app features:

  • The map can be zoomed in or out with either the zoom buttons or with the middle mouse scroll
  • Various things can be animated/highlighted when hovered on (or clicked with mobile devices)
  • The Interstate and Road icons can be clicked on to open a new page with relevant information
  • The FasTrak lanes in the key can be hovered on to highlight their corresponding lanes on the map (or clicked with mobile devices)
  • Dragging of the map can either be turned on or off
  • The merge structure of how the lanes merge down into five lanes can be shown or hidden
  • The app can have a fullscreen mode activated

Notes to user:

  • If on a mobile device, you must disable dragging before you can interact with the map
  • Mobile users might find the map easier to interact with if they zoom in some
  • The reset button will re-center the map and remove any zoom
